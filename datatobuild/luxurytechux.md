CZĘŚĆ 2: LEAD FORM OPTIMIZATION – Friction vs. Quality
Counter-Intuitive Finding: MORE Friction = BETTER Leads
Key Studies:

Study	Finding	Implication
Motive (CXL)	Added qualifying step → +20% CVR (not decrease)​	Longer forms = better qualified leads
Complice Case Study	Added fields → 90% fewer wasted sales calls (same quantity)​	Quality > quantity for €20k+
Sunk Cost Fallacy	Easy questions first → harder later → higher completion​	Psychology: investment = commitment
Why? Tire-kickers abandon at qualifying questions. Serious buyers complete longer forms to signal intent.

Optimal Lead Form Structure (MVP):
text
STEP 1: EASY ENTRY (Build Momentum – Sunk Cost)
Which robot interests you? (Dropdown: Tesla, Unitree, 1X, Not sure)

STEP 2: INTENT FILTER (Qualify Buyers)
Primary use case? (Elder care, Smart home, Research, Business, Other)

STEP 3: TIMELINE & BUDGET (Seriousness Indicator)
Timeline? (This month, Q1 2026, End 2026, Researching)
Budget? (€10k–25k, €25k–50k, €50k+, Prefer not to say)

STEP 4: CONTACT (Save Personal Data for Last)
First Name, Email, Phone (optional), Company, Country
✅ Privacy agreement
✅ Subscribe to updates (checkbox)
Form Optimization Tactics to A/B Test:

Button color: Blue vs. Green → Green wins (+3–5%)​

CTA copy: "Submit" vs. "Get Recommendation" → Variant wins

Privacy assurance: Add small trust badge → +8–12%​

Form length: 4-step vs. 2-step → 4-step wins (better quality)​
